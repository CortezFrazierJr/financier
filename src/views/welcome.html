About

This project started because I enjoy the UI and offline nature of YNAB 4. I do not like nYNAB's subscription billing model, and in my opinion the UI obfuscates the budget.

100% offline web app

If you're reading this text, this app is now on your computer. Try removing your internet connection and refreshing the page! Easy, right?

By being a web app, this app works across all platforms -- Windows, Mac OS X, or Linux. Just make sure you have a modern browser.

Except Safari. Safari is the new IE. Let's just pretend that Safari doesn't exist.

Free and open source

Financier is 100% free, forever. You can view and contribute to the source on Github.

In the future, I may offer a paid version that syncs with a server to provide your budget across devices.

How to use

If you're familiar with YNAB 4, using Financier should be straightforward. There are three main views: budget, accounts and reports.

Budget is the main dashboard for viewing month-to-month budgets. Here you can edit the budget for your various categories and see how you're doing.

Accounts is where you enter transactions and income as they show up in your bank account(s) or credit card(s). After you add a transaction or income, head back on over to budget to budget your income or assess your budget.

Reports is where you can see trends over the span of many months in various visualizations.
